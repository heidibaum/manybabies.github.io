Iberoamerican Congress on Open Science

Call for Spanish-speaking MB members


Nicolás Alessandroni and Krista Byers-Heinlein (Concordia University, Canada) are working on an oral communication proposal about ManyBabies for the 1st Iberoamerican Congress on Open Science. The presentation will be in Spanish and focus on introducing ManyBabies and its different projects to the Iberoamerican community, highlighting how big team science collaborations can contribute to fostering the implementation of open science practices. If you are a Spanish speaker and would like to collaborate in preparing the proposal, please contact Nicolás before September 8.

project updates

MB2: Theory of Mind

MB2 is ready to begin data collection! To learn more about the project, read the MB2 Stage 1 Registered Report (given an ‘In Principle Acceptance’ by Child Development) and the MB2 lab manual.

If you are interested in participating in data collection, complete your official data collection sign-up here. Contact Tobi Schuwerk with questions.

MB3: Rule Learning

MB3 is ready to begin data collection! For more details, read the MB3 Stage 1 Registered Report (given an 'In Principle Acceptance' by Developmental Science) and the MB3 lab manual.

We welcome labs to participate in data collection, please sign up here!

We are also looking for experienced labs that are willing to mentor other labs in setting up this study. Please let us know if you are willing to take on such a role by sending an email to Ingmar Visser. You can also email Ingmar with questions or would like to contribute in another way.

MB3N: Rule Learning fNIRS

After 4 years of no in person conferences, the Society for functional Near-Infrared Spectroscopy is holding its next meeting in Boston, USA, between 8-12 Oct 2022. The MB3N team will be presenting the project to the broader fNIRS community, hoping to spread the word not only about the project, but about replication and open science practices as well.

Make sure you are subscribed to the MB3N listserv to receive updates and info about project meetings.

MB4: Social Evaluation

MB4 has launched data collection and we invite new labs to join us! Sign up at this link and please help us spread the word. This form is different than our earlier “in principle” sign up - if you are unsure if you completed the official sign up you can contact Kelsey. Information about participating is found in the MB4 Big Manual. We ask that labs collect a minimum of 16 infants, and complete data collection by August 2023. Contact Kelsey Lucca or Francis Yuen with questions.

MB5: Hunter & Ames Model of Infant Looking Preference

Work continues incorporating contributor feedback into the registered report. The stimulus design team is hard at work finalizing stimuli – to get involved or learn more, check out the #stimulus_design channel of the MB5 Slack.

MB6: Neonatal Imitation

Thanks to everyone who participated in and attended the planning workshops for our newest project! Recordings of both workshops are available via the MB6 webpage. Make sure to subscribe to the MB6 listserv for updates about next steps. 

MB-AtHome: Online Infant Data Collection

Final preparations for testing via Lookit are ongoing. If you can help with translations, please contact Sho Tsuji.
