April 2022

In an effort to make MB leadership more representative of our community, we are increasing the size of the Governing Board (GB) from seven to nine members and creating the new role of Student Advisor (SA). We welcome GB nominees of any career level and GB/SA nominees from any region, but we especially encourage nominees from groups currently under- or not represented in MB general leadership (e.g., located outside of US/Canada, non-white, non-tenured; please see link below for more information). 

Please submit nominations (of yourself or others) to join MB’s general leadership as a member of the Governing Board (2 seats) or in the newly created role of Student Advisor (SA).

conferences and funding opportunities

The Open Science @Concordia conference will bring together advocates, enthusiasts, and other stakeholders in open science from across Concordia’s faculties and other regional institutions. Events include keynote talks featuring international speakers, interdisciplinary lightning-talk sessions, and lunch time roundtables. Attendees are welcome from any institution. Join either virtually or in person at the Loyola Jesuit Hall and Conference Centre (Montréal, Canada). The conference schedule and (free) registration form are available at https://crblm.ca/openscienceatconcordia/.

International Congress of Infant Studies: ICIS is scheduled to take place July 7-10 in Ottawa, Canada. Early bird registration deadline is May 2!
 

Lancaster Conference on Infant and Early Child Development: The deadline to submit abstracts has been extended to May 1.
 

SRCD SECC Dissertation Award: Apply by May 1.
 

SRCD Patrice L. Engle Dissertation Grant: Apply by June 30.
 

project updates

ATTENTION ALL MB CONTRIBUTORS!

If you are contributing to an active MB project, we ask that you fill out your project’s CRediT Form to report your contributions as soon as possible. You will be able to update your contribution report throughout the course of the project, and so responses should reflect actual contributions so far and/or plans to collect data (but not other planned contributions). Your responses will help Project Leads manage project authorship as well as make you eligible to vote in our upcoming Governing Board election. Links to CRediT Forms can be found in the project updates below.

MB1L (longitudinal CDI follow-up) is hard at work on final data analyses and manuscript preparation. Fill out the CRediT Form ASAP to ensure you get credit for your contributions.

committees

We are planning to gradually form committees to help handle specific needs. If you are interested in learning more about committee opportunities for one of the following MB committees (or have an idea for additional committees), please contact manybabiesconsortium@gmail.com. 

Diversity and Outreach: coordinate MB’s efforts to increase diversity of membership and testing populations

Communications: assist with the creation of the MB newsletter, manage MB social media accounts, coordinate press releases

Statistics and Analysis: consult with project analysis teams on analysis methodology (e.g., model specification, power analyses)

Software and Tech Implementation: coordinate and manage centralized MB resources (e.g., data validator, OSF) and consult with project leads and contributors regarding tech issues and implementation

Ethics: help coordinate project ethics considerations and submissions, consult with MB contributors about specific issues with obtaining ethics approval, manage data sharing policies and efforts
