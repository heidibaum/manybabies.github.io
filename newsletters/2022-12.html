announcements

Jacobs Foundation Research Fellowship Program


This fellowship program is geared towards early and mid-career researchers whose work is dedicated to improving the learning and development of children and youth worldwide. Learn more here.

Submission deadline: January 15, 2023, 23:59 CET
 

ICIS call for mentors


Anyone who is a regular member of ICIS can be a mentor for the Founding Generation Summer Fellowship. If you are interested in mentoring an undergraduate student for an 8-week summer internship, sign up here by January 13, 2023.

#devpsych Journal Club


Nikita Ghodke and Munna R. Shainy recently launched the #devpsych Journal Club, which aims to spread awareness of developmental psychology as a field, promote its interdisciplinarity, and uplift underrepresented researchers worldwide. Sign up here if you're interested in presenting or participating!

ManyBabies YouTube channel


You can watch recordings of the MB: Intro to Open Science workshop and talks from the Big Team Science Conference given by members of MB2, MB3, MB3-NIRS, MB4, MB5, MB-Demographics, & MB-AtHome on our YouTube channel! 

We'll be adding more content in the new year, so make sure to subscribe!

project updates

MB1T: Test-Retest spin-off

A full draft of the manuscript has been shared with project collaborators and will be submitted to Developmental Science shortly. If you involved in the project, please report contributions (and grant information, if applicable), read the draft, and approve its submission on the author spreadsheet by December 22.

MB2: Theory of Mind

Info about the MB2P: Pupil spin-off is now available on the website. Learn more here.

MB4: Social Evaluation

We are still looking for new contributors to collect data with us! If you are interested, please check out our website and complete our sign up form.

We are thrilled to announce that we have received ethics approval from the University of British Columbia to host a MB4 video database. 

Hilal Şen will present pilot data from an MB4 spin off project at the upcoming BCCCD in Budapest Jan 5-7th 2023.

MB5: Hunter & Ames Model of Infant Looking Preference

The Stage 1 Registered Report will be submitted shortly to Nature Human Behaviour. Thank you to everyone who contributed in the most recent round of feedback and editing! Also, make sure you have reported contributions via the MB5 CRediT form to be included as an author.
